Step 5: Maintenance & Safety Essentials

Regular maintenance is non-negotiable for machine longevity and operator safety. Follow these guidelines to keep your investment performing.

Daily Checks

  • ● Inspect tire pressure and tread.
  • ● Check for fluid leaks (hydraulic, engine oil, coolant).
  • ● Verify all lights, horn, and safety alarms are functioning.
  • ● Clear debris from the engine and radiator.
  • ● Confirm all hoses and belts are in good condition.

Weekly Maintenance

  • ● Lubricate all grease points.
  • ● Check fluid levels (hydraulic, transmission, axle).
  • ● Inspect air filter and clean or replace as needed.
  • ● Check battery terminals for corrosion.
  • ● Inspect backhoe and loader pins and bushings.

Step 7: Get Your Questions Answered

Common questions from procurement managers and engineers.

What is the best size backhoe for road construction?

A model with 80-95 HP, a digging depth of 14-16 feet, and a 1.2 m³ bucket capacity is typically ideal for the varied tasks of road construction, from trenching to material loading.

How important is 4-wheel drive (4WD) in Africa?

It is critically important. 4WD provides essential traction, stability, and pushing power on the unpaved, uneven, and often challenging terrains common in African road projects, ensuring safety and operational efficiency.
